The Game

New here? This section is the whole story in ninety seconds. Everything below it is just the current state of play.
Why — the stakes

Right now, how QPC does its work lives in people's heads.

Heads get promoted, retire, and leave. Every departure deletes knowledge; every new hire learns by rumor. A second shift can't launch on word-of-mouth, audits get harder every cycle, and the aerospace standard we certify to literally requires capturing this knowledge before it walks out the door. And the timing matters: demand for what QPC does is surging while qualified suppliers stay scarce — the companies that can prove how they work are the ones that get to grow into it.

What — the build

A qualification system, not a binder of handouts.

About 35 short courses ("modules") covering every job in the building. Each one works in three beats: KNOW the rules and why they exist, DO the work in front of a qualified evaluator, PROVE it with a test and a signed, recorded checkout. People climb a ladder — trainee → working under supervision → trusted to work independently → qualified to evaluate others. And nobody, at any level, ever signs off their own work.

So what — the win condition

Version 1.0 ships when five things are true.

Every position in the company has a training path. Every fact in the training traces to an official document or an owner's recorded decision. Quality has approved the passing scores and the instant-fail lists. One module has been walked through the official paperwork pipeline as a successful test run. And every shift has enough qualified evaluators to actually run it. That's the whole game — everything on this board serves one of those five.

Rules of play — how this build stays honest

  1. The written plan outranks memory. Meetings, hallway answers, and recollections are input. When they disagree with a written, controlled document, the document wins until the build owner resolves the conflict.
  2. Facts have grades. Official documents can be taught as fact. Things people said in meetings wait until they're confirmed. Open questions get written down as questions — never dressed up as answers.
  3. Training level, job title, and pay are three separate things. This program owns only the first one. Finishing a module never silently changes anyone's title or paycheck.
  4. Nobody verifies their own work. Every checkout is observed and signed by a qualified, independent evaluator. That's stricter than the industry requires — on purpose.
  5. An honest gap beats a polished guess. Drafts go out with their open questions flagged in plain sight, every time.

The Dream — what's on the other side of done

Everything below this section is weeds and weed-cutting. This section is the cleared field. Six doors, all locked today, all opened by the same key — and none of them is hype: each one traces to a real constraint this build has already touched.

A company that survives its people leaving

Today, when someone walks out, their knowledge walks out with them. With v1.0, the knowledge stays: a new hire starts Monday with a defined path, a qualified verifier, and a record of every step — productive in weeks instead of "a year of shadowing whoever has time."

How we live: vacations get taken. Promotions don't create craters. Nobody is irreplaceable in the dangerous way.

The second shift

It's gated on training readiness right now — that's not a metaphor, it's the actual blocker. The qualification system is the literal key: defined paths, enough verifiers on every shift, a night crew that runs on the same standard as days instead of a rumor of it.

How we live: capacity roughly doubles inside the same four walls — without the chaos doubling with it.

Expansion becomes deployment

Washougal proved QPC grows. Today, opening a site means exporting your best people and praying. With a portable curriculum, the operation becomes a turnkey playbook — site #3 is a deployment: ship the system, qualify the people, run the checkouts.

How we live: grow where the customers are, without permanently losing the people who built this place.

Audits flip from threat to showcase

The next audit will look at exactly what this build owns — training, competence, records. With v1.0, QPC is the small shop that walks the auditor through a qualification ladder with signed proof behind every name. Same story in customer audits — the rooms where SpaceX, ULA, and Boeing approvals get won.

How we live: audit week stops being scramble week. Training records become a sales asset.

A ladder people can see

Every tech gets a visible path — trainee to evaluator, earned through demonstrated work, recorded and provable. "How do I move up?" gets an answer that isn't politics, isn't favoritism, isn't waiting for someone to notice you.

How we live: hiring gets easier to pitch, good people get easier to keep, and skill finally has a receipt.

A business worth more than its busiest week

QPC sits in a rare position: demand from reshoring, space and defense, and AI-infrastructure buildouts is surging — while certified suppliers stay scarce, because the approvals QPC already holds take years to earn. A company in that position that also runs on documented systems instead of specific heroic people can be valued, scaled, financed, or grown into every bit of that demand. Documentation maturity is enterprise value, and this curriculum is one of its load-bearing walls.

How we live: QPC becomes an asset that compounds — not a job that depends on who showed up.

The Two Gates

Only two things truly stand between "courses written" and "system running" — and neither is a writing task. Everything else on this board is sequencing toward these.
Enough qualified checkers on every shift

The rule that nobody signs off their own work only works if every shift has enough qualified people to do the checking. Before rollout, we count: how many verifiers exist, per shift, per department — and close any gap. The one-page Second-Shift Readiness Checklist (Move #8) is this gate's working face.

One successful test run through the official pipeline

Before any training document becomes official quality documentation, we walk exactly one — the shipping course and its checklist — through the full conversion process, end to end. If the pipeline works on one, it works on all. No mass conversion until the test run passes.

Closed — the receipts

Decided and done. Folded away because the past doesn't need screen space — open a drawer when someone asks "wait, when did we settle that?"
Eight questions only the Quality Manager could answer — all answered
  • Passing scores are set for every course, and the practical checkout must be passed — a good written test can't carry a failed hands-on.
  • The "instant-fail" list exists: a dozen mistakes so serious that making one fails the checkout and triggers retraining, no matter the rest of the score.
  • The exact words to use when reporting a suspect part — describe what you see, never declare a conclusion you can't prove.
  • The reporting chain for bad parts: the finder reports and protects the part; Quality owns the decision and the paperwork. Nobody on the floor decides a part's fate.
  • Who sits on the review board for problem parts, what decisions they may make, how the locked quarantine cage works, and when the customer must be told.
  • How inspection stamps are issued, tracked, and controlled.

  • How the production schedule really updates: technicians scan the job's label and the board updates itself — a chat message is the exception, not the system.
  • Which QR code is real: the old time-tracking code is retired; the label code on every job is the live one. (The training was corrected the same day we learned this.)
  • The right person to interview for the lab's knowledge has been identified by name and role — the interview itself is Move #1.
  • How cleaning solvents are controlled — answered by the master cleaning procedure; one labeling question remains.
